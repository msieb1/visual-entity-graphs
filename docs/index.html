Graph-Structed Visual Imitation

Maximilian Sieb*, Zhou Xian*, Audrey Huang, Oliver Kroemer , Katerina Fragkiadaki
Carnegie Mellon University


Visual entity graph (VEG) representation of human demonstration and robot imitation for two timesteps of a typical imitation. Corresponding nodes in the human demonstration (top) and robot imitation (bottom) share the same color. Our graphs are hierarchical. Edges exist between object and robot/human hand nodes and point feature nodes, and are added/deleted dynamically over time based on the motion saliency (as shown in the figure with solid/dashed lines).


Abstract

We cast visual imitation as a visual correspondence learning problem. We build upon multi-view self-supervised visual feature learning, synthetic data augmentation, and human finger detectors to learn time-tracked detectors for visual entities of various granularities in the teacher and student environments. We define perceptual similarity to be agreement of the relative spatial arrangements (3D distances) of corresponding visual entities across teacher and learner graphs. We use this perceptual similarity as a re ward function during trajectory optimization. We show the proposed image graph encoding drives successful imitation of a variety of manipulation skills within minutes, using a single demonstration and without any environment instrumentation. It generalizes across background clutter, perturbation of initial conditions, and even across different object instances, and greatly outperforms perceptual similarity functions built over convolutional image embeddings of previous works.


We use human hand keypoint detectors, multi-viewpoint feature learning, and synthetic image generation for on-the-fly object detector training from only a few object mask examples. Using a manually designed mapping between the human hand and the robot, the visual detectors can effectively bridge the visual gap between demonstrator and imitator environment, are robust to background clutter, and generalize across different object instances.
